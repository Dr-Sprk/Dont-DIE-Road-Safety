Distractions are a key cause of many accidents

What is Distracted Driving?

The actual Definition of distracted driving is when a driver participates in an activity not related to the actual operation of the vehicle while driving a car and the activity has the potential of shifting the driver's focus from their primary objective, which is to safely operate their vehicle while focusing on the road and traffic conditions. Distracted driving includes operating mobile devices while driving as well as other equally distracting situations or activities. Distracted driving activities increase the risk of having accidents and in a study, it is evident that distracted driving is a serious problem among teen drivers.


The Primary Causes

There are several causes that can lead to distracted driving and hence, a higher risk of an accident, however these are the primary culprits:

  • Texting
  • Talking on a mobile phone
  • Using a mobile device to use the internet while driving
  • Watching videos or using entertainment devices
  • Using social media or taking selfies or streaming live video
  • Grooming (combing hair, putting on makeup)
  • Eating
  • Partying in a car, having loud, distracting passengers or driving under emotional stress
  • Reading, visually reading directions or instructions, or email (Visual Distraction)


How should I stay concentrated and not distracted?

It is absolutely essential as a driver, especially as a new driver, to stay completely concentrated on the road at all times. Although challenging at times, it is definitely possible and people with the cheapest car insurance premiums are those who drive well and hence stay focused on the road ahead. Safe and good driving sans distractions can be achieved by keeping your phone out of arm's reach whenever you are driving, and putting it on its Do Not Disturb function to miniise the risk of notifications detracting from your attention. It is also important to keep loud music down to a minimum, and generally, to not listen to music. Infotainment systems in cars are also quite big distractions and their operation should be minimised whilst actively driving the car.
