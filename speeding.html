Don't Increase Your Speed over the Limit

Speeding in young drivers is a real problem, and is one of the most common causes of injury or death.

Learn More

What is exactly is speeding?

Speeding is one of the most known and common road mistakes, yet it is still the 'leading behavioural factor in death and injuries on our roads, contributing to about 40 per cent of road fatalities' according to NSW Road Safety. Statistics show that from speeding alone roughly 1500 people were seriously injured each year. At a point in time wherein speeding might seem like a thrill, you risk being killed.


The Impacts

Despite these consequences already being tragic, speeding accidents not only affects the driver. By increasing you speed above the limit, you risk not being able to have fast enough reation times should something come in the path of your car, e.g. a pedestrian, kangaroo, sudden stoppage of car in front. In fact, these are one of the leading causes of fatalities in speeding accidents. Furthermore, the detrimental impact that the injury or loss of a community member has upon the physically and socially surrounding people is absolutely terrible, and in some cases these people need to visit a psychologist for trauma treatment.
